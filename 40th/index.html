Four decades turning ideas into impacts

This year marks the 40th Anniversary of Energetics’ founding. For 40 years, we have been advising those at the forefront of energy technology, helping to shape the way that the world responds to changing energy demands and the reality of our environment. Whether in the realm of energy management or energy security, smart technology or environmental science, everyone at Energetics shares the passion that sparked the founding of this company in 1979.

A lot has changed since then. Energetics founders Jim Reed and Tom Bustard saw both need and opportunity: complex, universal challenges that required holistic solutions. Jim and Tom recognized energy’s consequence to a nation’s well-being and prosperity, and as keen analysts, they established Energetics to help clients tackle energy-based problems. Our first executives could not have foreseen exactly how critical a role power generation would play in an unknowable future, how energy would take center stage in the global conversation.

Over the years, our foundation in energy consulting has evolved into a capacity to help address some of today’s imperatives. Energetics has had the opportunity to work with clients in over a dozen nations, collaborating with federal, state, and local governments, as well as leaders in academia, NGOs, and industry. Our engineers, scientists, policy analysts, and social scientists have been honored to play roles in significant initiatives that have shaped our nation’s energy policy, advanced the use of alternative energy in multiple industries, and enhanced the resilience of our nation’s communities (check out the sidebar and 40th Anniversary timeline). We embrace these opportunities because our clientele have critical missions, remarkable visions, and tremendous responsibilities for the future.

Many thanks to all who have allowed us to work with you, side by side, toward a secure and resilient horizon. Here’s to another 40 years!

  • 1977 The newly formed Department of Energy consolidates the Federal Energy Administration, Energy Research and Development Administration, Federal Power Commission, and energy programs of other agencies.
  • 1979 Thomas Stratton Bustard and James Reed found Energetics Incorporated, with a vision to support the newly formed Department of Energy.

  • 1986 Major SBIR project undertaken to develop a knowledge-based expert system for a pulp/paper mill in Alabama.
  • 1987 Led the development of the first-ever Multi-year Plan for the Department of Energy’s Office of Conservation and Renewable Energy.
  • 1988 First energy profiles developed projecting future industrial energy use in steel, pulp/paper, glass, cement, and textiles.
  • 1988 Thirty employees share two personal computers and a single report paginator. Email is non-existent; library visits and hardcover reports provide data for analysis. Reports are laid out on a drafting table, with printouts of typed text glued onto pages.

Founders Profile

Dr. Thomas Bustard served in the US Coastguard before turning to energy consulting; he held a MS from Drexel University and a PhD in chemical engineering from University of Maryland. Dr. Bustard passed away in early 2019 after a long and illustrious career.

James E. Reed had a passion for energy starting early in his career. He holds an MS, Electrical Science and Applied Physics from Case Western Reserve University, and a BS in Engineering Science from Pennsylvania State University. He retired as President in 2010 and is now enjoying his retirement in Florida.

American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009

The American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009 (ARRA) is signed into law in February 2009. It includes measures to modernize the nation’s energy infrastructure including $4.5 billion to modernize the electric power grid. In its largest program, the Smart Grid Investment Grant (SGIG), DOE and the electricity industry jointly invest $8 billion in 99 cost-shared projects involving more than 200 participating electric utilities.

Clean Energy Ministerial

Energetics has supported the CEM from its start in 2010 – showing our passion for a sustainable world. In 2019, the tenth Clean Energy Ministerial (CEM10) took up four universal, key themes central to advancing clean energy: Clean Power, Smarter Use of Energy, Sustainable Clean Energy Financing, and Workforce and Communities.
